Pieces

- All pieces in Twelve Showgi can only move one square per turn.

- King: can move in all directions.

- Rook: can move forward, backward, left, and right.

- Bishop: can move in four diagonal directions.

- Pawn: can move only forward. Note that pawns can capture pieces directly in front of them, and can never move diagonally, unlike chess. when a pawn reaches the end of the board, it promotes to a queen.

- Queen: can move in every direction, except the backward two diagonals.

Rules

- The front three tiles is your side.

- White goes first. One move per turn is allowed. If you capture a piece, you can use the piece against the opponent by placing it on the board when you wish.

- Placing down a captured piece also consumes a turn. You can't place a piece where a piece is already present, or the opponent's first three tiles.

- When you capture the opponent's queen, and wish to use it, you have to place it as a pawn.

- The game ends when a player captures the opponent's king, or when a player's king enters the opponent's first three tiles, and not get captured until the start of the next turn.

Fun facts

- This game was introduced in the Korean TV show "The Genius", and its origin is Animal Showgi from Japan.

- The pieces are not supposed to be chess pieces, but I did it because of convenience.

- Do not use SVG components. You will hate yourself.
